Frequently Asked Questions

What is Taiko drumming?

Taiko is a traditional form of Japanese percussion that uses large, hollow wooden drums. At Tamashii Taiko Drummers, we perform a contemporary style of Taiko. This means we take traditional Japanese rhythms and fuse them with modern choreography and powerful, athletic movements to create a highly visual and energetic musical experience.

Do I need musical or drumming experience to join a class?

Not at all! Our beginner introductory courses are designed for people with absolutely no prior musical or drumming background. We teach you everything from the ground up, including proper stance, how to hold the bachi (drumsticks), and basic rhythms. Just bring a water bottle, comfortable workout clothes, and plenty of energy.

Where are your Taiko classes located?

Our main studio and practice space is located at 2B Malvern Road, Onehunga, Auckland. We also travel across the wider Auckland region to host dedicated corporate team-building workshops and school educational programs at your own venue.

What types of events do you perform at?

We perform at a wide variety of events across Auckland and New Zealand. Our premier squad frequently opens for large-scale corporate functions, gala dinners, and award ceremonies with explosive, highly choreographed sets. We also regularly perform at local community festivals, charity fundraisers, and cultural celebrations, using scalable group sizes to fit different venues and budgets.

Are you a commercial business or a community group?

Tamashii Taiko is proudly registered as a not-for-profit Incorporated Society in New Zealand. Our primary mission is to promote Japanese Taiko drumming and related performing arts. When you hire us for a corporate event or join our classes, your investment goes directly back into maintaining our instruments, training our performers, and keeping this vibrant cultural art form accessible to the Auckland community.
